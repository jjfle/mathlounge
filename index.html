Polls.

The use of live interactive polls is a powerful tool for enabling hands-on learning in the classroom.
It is not only valuable for learning, but also can be used as a method of formative assessment.

FLUX is a powerful tool for creating polls for classrooms. It engages students with the content of the class through: word clouds, media polls or free test response prompts and responses.
The polls are easily shared between others, allowing for consistent learning experiences across classes. Students can contribute to the polls using their devices, and quickly provide insights or gain feedback to problems.
Students can easily join the session using the generated code for each room.

Game-Based Learning.

Gamification is the process of implementing game-like elements within the classroom to motivate and engage students in activities.

Blooket reimagines learning by gamifying quizzes and trivia. As the teacher you can create or choose an existing set of questions related to the content you are covering in class as well as a game mode.
Students can then join the session using their devices via a generated code.

Blooket has been shown to enhance students' motivation in learning, due to being a fun, competitive and by promoting understanding of content.

Mathematical Tools.

Mathematical tools are powerful in aiding in students development of understanding. The ability to visualise and interact with problems helps students engage with the material.

Geogebra is a platform that combines the capabilities of a graphics calculator and the resources of textbook.
Geogebra's calculator allows students to plot and visualise functions in both 2-dimensions and 3-dimensions, create, analyse and annotate geometric figures, access a CAS calculator, and analyse and visualise various probability distributions.
Geogebra's math practice feature guides students through the steps or methods to solve routine problems such as arithmetic, equation solving and simplifying expressions.
Geogebra also allow students to join lessons and collaborate with one another to solve challenging problems or to share observations and insights.

Geogebra has been shown to benefit students mathematics within the classroom.

Mathematics Digital Languages.

Many students often find mathematics to be a written language that doesn't mesh well the digital world. For students with poor handwriting it can make maths difficult to engage with, in terms of completing work.
There are also many students that wish to write digital summaries or implement mathematical notation into other subjects that benefit from digital write-ups, often struggle to do so.

LaTeX is a well known language which excels at displaying mathematical notation using simple commands. Overleaf - an online LaTeX editor - combines the skills related to code and visual editting into a single experience, while allowing for multiple users to collaborate on the same document.
As such students can sharpen multiple skills simultaneously.

As teachers, the use of LaTeX also assists us in developing accessible notes or documentation for students, particularly for assistive technologies such as screen readers.

Within the later years of high school, students have shown few difficulties learning LaTeX, with most eager to implement it in lab reports and homework, as such the skills can be applied across multiple subjects.
For students pursuing tertiary education, being capable of understanding and using LaTeX is very beneficial for assignments and online discussion, making the skillset useful beyond the classroom.
